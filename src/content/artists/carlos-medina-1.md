short_bio: Carlos Medina graduated from the Escuela de Artes Visuales in Caracas
  (1975), continued his formation at the Professional School of Marble in
  Carrara, Italy, from 1977, with further studies in bronze at Fonderia Mariani,
  Pietrasanta, and printmaking at the Rijkscentrum Frans Masereel, Kasterlee,
  Belgium.
---
Medina has held solo exhibitions at institutions such as the Museum of Contemporary Art of Caracas (1975), Galerie Denise René et Palais Royal Paris (since 2017) His work has been awarded more than thirty prizes and distinctions, including the prize for the artistic intervention of the Gare de Bussy-Saint-Georges, France (2019), in collaboration with Atelier Cruz-Diez and includes around forty public sculptures in different countries, notably “Fragment of Rain for Caracas.“
