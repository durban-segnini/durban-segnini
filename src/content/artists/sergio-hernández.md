Born in Mexico, Hernández pursued his artistic studies at the National School of Painting, Sculpture, and Engraving "La Esmeralda" in Mexico City, as well as at the National School of Plastic Arts at the Academy of San Carlos, National Autonomous University of Mexico. He received multiple scholarships, including from the National Council for Culture and the Arts and the National Fund for Culture and the Arts, which supported his artistic development. Hernández’s unique style gained international recognition through exhibitions in France, Italy, Germany, and Spain, cementing his reputation as one of the leading figures in contemporary Mexican art.

Throughout his career, Hernández has received numerous recognitions, including the René Portocarrero International Award in 2014 and the Cross of Officer of the Order of Isabel la Católica in 2006. He has also won several awards at the Palace of Fine Arts in Mexico City and national competitions such as the National Painting Contest for Students of Plastic Arts. His works have been showcased in prestigious museums and galleries worldwide, including the Museum of Modern Art in Mexico City, the Museum of Santander in Texas, and the Musée du Quai Branly in Paris, where his installation *Tzompantli* was exhibited.

Hernández’s art continues to explore the intersection of tradition and contemporary themes, reflecting his deep connection to the land, culture, and history of Oaxaca. His works are held in prominent collections, making him a key figure in the modern Mexican art scene.
