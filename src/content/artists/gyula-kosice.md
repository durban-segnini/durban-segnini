Kosice became a prominent member of the Madí movement in 1946, alongside artists like Carmelo Arden Quin, Martín Blaszko, and Rhod Rothfuss. He was instrumental in promoting the group internationally and founded *Arte Madí Universal* magazine. He played a central role within the group, organizing exhibitions and promoting their international presence. In 1948, Kosice showcased the group's work at the *Salon Réalités Nouvelles* in Paris. Kosice’s work focused on transforming art from static objects into dynamic, participatory experiences. His *Escultura Móvil Articulada* (1948), made from materials used in leatherworking, was a precursor to mobile art, allowing viewers to rearrange the sculpture. His later works incorporated neon lights and flowing water, merging technology and art in unique ways. 

Throughout his career, Kosice created several groundbreaking exhibitions. Notable solo shows included *Spatial Constructions and First Hydrospatial Sculpture* at Drian Gallery in London (1953) and *Hydrospatial City* at the Espace Cardin in Paris (1975). His *Hydrospatial City* was *created* in 1947— a conceptual project exploring the potential for art and architecture to be fluid, interactive, and ever-changing. This interactive structure made of water and platforms was presented in various versions over the years, including installations in Buenos Aires and Tokyo. 

He also presented his work at major biennials, including the XXVIII International Biennial Exhibition of Venice (1956), the IV and VI International Biennial Exhibitions of São Paulo (1957 and 1961), the III Coltejer Biennial in Medellín (1972), and the II Biennial Exhibition of Havana (1986). His works are celebrated in major museums, including the Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes in Buenos Aires. Kosice passed away in 2016 in Buenos Aires, leaving behind a legacy of innovation in kinetic and interactive art that continues to inspire through their exploration of space and mobility.
